Water Cycle Explained

🌊 Evaporation

The sun's energy heats water in oceans and rivers, turning it into water vapor (a gas), which rises into the atmosphere.

☁️ Condensation

As water vapor rises, it cools and changes back into tiny liquid water droplets, forming clouds.

🌧️ Precipitation

When clouds become too full of water droplets, they release the water back to Earth as rain, snow, or hail.

🏞️ Collection

Water that falls on land flows over the surface (runoff) or soaks into the ground (infiltration), eventually collecting in rivers, lakes, and oceans.
